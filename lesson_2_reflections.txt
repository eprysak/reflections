What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?

A .git folder is created. The files in the folder are needed for configuring the git settings for that repository.

How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it offers?

The staging area is more temporary and allows flexibility in changing the contents, while the working directory and repository are more permanent.

How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?

You can use the staging area to review the specific files that need the logical change and only include those.

What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organized? How would branches help?

When you want to try different versions of your program, or are testing something experimental, you should use branches.

How do the diagrams help you visualize the branch structure?

The diagrams show the different branches, where they branch off, and what commits are reachable or not.

What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in the diagram the way we do?

Merging the two branches together allows for two paths of code to be combined into one so that changes made by multiple people can be saved into the final version.
